Part 2: Scope of Services Offered

If an organization does not directly provide any of the four services listed below, the services should be available through local partnerships with organizations that offer such services.

  • Pre-Purchase Education & Counseling
    Has the organization implemented a homeownership education and counseling program that meets the minimum requirements (8+ hours of training or counseling) of the National Homeownership Programs?
    If the NWO contracts homeownership education and counseling to another organization, does the NWO provide quality control over the program to ensure it meets the National Homeownership Programs standards?
    Does the NWO have current Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) in place with a partnering organization that is offering pre-purchase education and counseling services?
  • Flexible Lending Products
    Is affordable financing (including second or third mortgages or grant programs) provided directly or through partners to meet the lending needs of customers for down payment assistance, closing costs, or rehabilitation financing?
    If the NWO does not directly offer flexible lending products, does the NWO have current Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) in place with a partnering organization that is offering flexible lending products?
  • Portfolio Management
    Does the portfolio have delinquency and default rates (# of loans and $ amounts) comparable to the NWO’s internal risk rating system or Neighborworks America’s benchmark of ten percent?
    Does the NWO have a current Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) in place with a partnering organization that is offering portfolio management services (if applicable)?
  • Post-Purchase Education and Counseling
    Does the NWO provide an annual series of post-purchase seminars for homeowners on topics such as home maintenance, financial capability, and other related topics?
    Is individual budget, credit counseling, and financial coaching available either directly or through referrals to assist homeowners in financial difficulties?

Compliance and Verification Checklist

  • 1. NWO mission is compatible with that of NeighborWorks America
  • 2. NWO develops, manages, and maintains programs and services that are at all times in keeping with that mission.
  • 3. NWO has established and maintains a Board of Directors that reflects a partnership among resident leaders, the private business community and public officials in which at least one-third of the Board members are of low-income residents or low-income resident representatives of the NWO's service area. Resident board members can be:
    • a resident of a low income community within the NWO's service
    • a low-income resident within the NWO's service area, or
    • an individual who is elected or appointed to represent residents of low-income communities in the NWO service area
  • 4. NWO is in good standing as a non-profit organizaton per the applicable laws and regulations of the original chartering state.
  • 5. NWO qualifies for and maintains its federal non-profit status per 501(c)(3) of the IRS Code, and is not controlled by any other organization.
  • 6. NWO has established and maintains a financial management system that includes adequate internal controls and is otherwise compliant with Section VI(a) of the NeighborWorks® America Grant Agreement.
  • 7. NWO has a conflict of interest policy covering its directors, officers and staff that was adopted through the Board of Directors.
  • 8. NWO has an unqualified audit done by a CPA persuant to the NWO Guidance for the Conduct of Audit
  • 9. NWO has submitted the required consolidated audit to NeighborWorks® America.
  • 10. NWO submits to NWA the geographic boundaries of the NWO's service area and seeks the written approval of the NWA Regional VP if the service area is changed or expanded.
  • 11. NWO participates and cooperates fully in regularly scheduled Program Reviews (including any subsidiaries or related organizations referred to as sub-recipients) conducted by NeighborWorks® America or its designee.
  • 12. NWO develops and implements a Corrective Action plan to correct significant deficiencies identifed during the Program Review.
  • 13. NWO clearly and consistently conveys membership in the NeighborWorks® network by use of the NeighborWorks® Chartered Member logo and through descriptive language and text.

Summary Checklist for HomeOwnership Center Standards

  • NeighborWorks Homeownership Center Orientation
    Has the HomeOwnership Center Manager or Executive Director attended and completed the NeighborWorks® homeownership center orientation?
  • Leadership
    Has the Center recruited an advisory committee representing all stakeholders to provide direction and oversight for the HomeOwnership Center during the development and start-up phases of implementation? (Such an advisory committee is not required after operations have begun.)
    Does the Center have clear criteria for membership onto its advisory committee and a mechanism in place to rotate and renew its membership periodically?
  • Staffing
    Does the Center have a manager separate from the Executive Director of the NWO?
    Does the Center employ at least three professional staff?
  • Production and Full-Cycle Lending Certification
    Does the Center meet the Campaign's minimum production of 40 homeowners per year and does it maintain full-cycle lending certification on an ongoing basis?
  • Customer Service Evaluation or Surveys
    Does the Center utilize a specific mechanism to monitor the quality of services delivered for both homeownership education training and other products and services? (E. g., does it have a customer survey form for measuring the level of satisfaction both at the end of the HBE training and at the end of the homebuying process? Does it have a customer survey form for measuring the quality of postpurchase services?)
    Does the Center either directly or through partners provide access to the range of services needed by prospective homebuyers and existing homeowners?
  • Location
    Does the Center operate out of a highly visible retail location with space dedicated exclusively to the Center?
  • Design Standards
    Does the Center utilize the standard signage and design components as required for all NeighborWorks homeownership Centers?
